About Us

Ereto Namunyak is a local organization with a membership of 10 drawn from a wide range of professions. Ereto Namunyak was formed in 2024 and registered in 2025 as a community-based organization under the laws of Kenya. The vision of Ereto Namunyak is to have a rising, prosperous Maa community where everyone has a share and a role to play. The mission is to fight hunger, drought, illiteracy, and bad governance. The formation of the organization was precipitated by the realization among professionals that there were deficiencies regarding socio-economic development in the community, particularly in education, infrastructure, human resource development, and land/pastoral resource base protection. Consequently, the core problems that were the focus of the organization’s efforts from the onset were low education standards, spearheading the rights of persons with special needs, primary health care, and educating the society to acknowledge their rights and responsibilities. Ereto-Namunyak's operations cover the whole of Magadi, Shompole and Olkeri community land, Oldonyo Nyokie and Olkiramatian group ranches.

Key Programs

Our Mission

To foster positive changes among community groups and the community at large,specifically to;

  • Enlighten the community on matters pertaining to the society, making good use of readily available resources to create wealth in the community.
  • Fight hunger, drought, illiteracy, and bad governance.
  • Create awareness in the community on matters of education, church empowerment, gender equality, FGM and HIV/AIDS.
  • Educate society to acknowledge their rights and responsibilities.
  • Spearhead the rights of persons with special needs.

Our Projects

Women Empowerment Program

Women are an integral part of the community, yet many face significant challenges including poverty, illiteracy, and a lack of recognition due to cultural norms. Our CBO is committed to addressing these issues, with a special focus on supporting widows and vulnerable women.

  • We create community awareness about the vital role of women and encourage them to pursue their rights and seek leadership positions.
  • We conduct literacy programs to provide basic education, empowering women to improve their lives and those of their families.
  • We integrate women into income-generating activities (IGA) to combat poverty and foster financial self-reliance.

Persons With Special Needs Empowerment Program

In collaboration with our partners, we are dedicated to supporting children with special needs (under 18) and their families. This program focuses on providing comprehensive care and creating opportunities for growth.

  • Providing specialized training for the caregivers of children with special needs.
  • Supplying nutritional supplements to ensure the health and well-being of the children.
  • Offering educational sponsorships to ensure every child has access to learning.

Church Empowerment Program

We believe that strong spiritual institutions build strong communities. This program aims to empower local churches by providing essential resources and training to their leaders and congregations.

  • Leadership Training: We offer training programs for pastors, deacons, and other church leaders in advocacy, discipleship, and integrity to meet the evolving needs of their ministries.
  • Resources and Infrastructure: We collaborate with partners to provide essential church stationery and reading materials. When resources permit, we also assist in building physical church structures, helping congregations move from worshiping under trees to having a dedicated place of fellowship.

Environmental Conservation and Climate Change Initiatives

Our initiative focuses on promoting environmental sustainability and combating the effects of climate change to secure a healthier future for the community and its livestock.

  • We facilitate the sourcing of tree seedlings and encourage tree planting in schools, homes, and public areas.
  • We support the sourcing and planting of fodder in farming-conducive areas to ensure availability during dry seasons.
  • We promote local tourism and culture by preserving cultural architecture and conserving local flora and fauna.

Education and Youth Empowerment

  • Life Skills Training: Offering training in communication, problem-solving, and financial literacy to empower youth to navigate life's challenges.
  • Mentorship Programs: Connecting youth with successful adult mentors for personal and professional guidance and support.
  • Advocacy: Actively working with local authorities to eradicate FGM, early marriages, and school dropouts, which threaten the educational future of our community.
  • Leadership Development: Providing leadership training to encourage youth to become active agents of change in their communities.

Gender Equality

We are committed to promoting gender equality by challenging discriminatory norms and empowering all members of our community. Our impact is focused on two key areas:

  • Advocacy and Awareness: We engage in advocacy to raise awareness about the importance of gender equality, working to challenge societal stereotypes that perpetuate discrimination.
  • Education and Training: We offer educational programs and workshops to empower women and marginalized genders, including skills training, leadership development, and financial literacy to foster independence.
